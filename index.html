Why our Founding Father Benjamin Franklin was great

Benjamin Franklin one of our Founding Farthers was a polymath, inventor, scientist, printer, politician, freemason and diplomat.Franklin helped to draft the Declaration of Independence and the U.S. Constitution, and he negotiated the 1783 Treaty of Paris ending the Revolutionary War. Franklin had the first several slaves working for him at his home in 1748 but later free them in the 1760s. His views on slavery changed and he wrote many tracts urging the abolition of slavery.

Timeline of Benjamin Franklin life:

  • Jan.1706-Benjamin Franklin was born
  • 1727-Suffers first pleurisy attack
  • 1732-Birth of his son Francis Folger
  • 1734-Is elected Grand Master of the Grand Masonic Lodge of Masons of PA
  • 1736-Son Francis Folger dies at age 4 by smallpox
  • 1740-Buys 5,000 acres to built school for African Americans (school not built)
  • 1752-Received Copley Medal of the royal Society of London for research in electricity. Deputy Postmaster General of N.A.
  • 1759-Receives honorary degree of Doctor of Laws from the University of St. Andrews, Scotland
  • 1771-Begins writing his Autobiography
  • 1776-Presides over Constitutional Convention of PA.
  • 1783-Signed Peace Treaty
  • 1789-He becomes president of the Society for Promoting the Abolition of Slavery
  • Apr.17 1790 -Dies in Philadelphia at the age of 84
